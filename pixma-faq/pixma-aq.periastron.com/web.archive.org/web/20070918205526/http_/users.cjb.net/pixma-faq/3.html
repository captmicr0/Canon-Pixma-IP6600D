  • Making a basic tray


    fig 3-3 by digiguy

    Click above for full sized printable

    version.

    To make your basic tray you'll need the following items


    fig 3-4 by knightcrawler


    1. Template. See fig 3-3.

    2. Two pieces of cardboard or poster board measuring 131mm by 239mm

    3. One x-acto knife or equivalent

    4. Glue

    5. Tape

    6. Metric ruler

    7. Paper Chopper / optional.

    8. Black Magic marker

    9. Scrap CD

    10. Tin foil or scrap paper.




    Instructions are rather simple.

      1. First of all print out digiguy's template at 300dpi using your favorite application.

      2. Tape 1 copy to a piece of 2.5mm / .1 in. cardboard/poster board. If in doubt about the thickness it's about the same as a CD but no smaller.

      3. Using a ruler as your guide take your X-acto knife or equivalent and cut just before the black lines to make the along the outer edge. This can also be accomplished with a paper chopper. Measure the width and check by seeing if the cardboard is not too wide to insert into your printer.

      4. Cut the for squares at the top of the print out. They should measure 5.5mm square. Blacken the area around them with a magic market.

      5. Using a scrap CD as your guide align it with the circle and cut a round circle

      6. Remove the tape and tape your template to the other piece of cardboard and repeat step one. You might use a freshly printed copy. Thickness of the base layer should be 1.143mm .045 inch or slightly smaller than a CD.

      7. Cut two pieces of tin foil or white paper ½ inch square and ½ inch x 1½ inch. Tape this to the bottom side of your top layer. Alternatively white spray oil based primer can be used

      8. Glue top and bottom together using general purpose glue. Let dry

      9. Test using CD-label print or equivalent application. Also see Section [7] for testing and alignment.

  • Making a tray with hub

    This is like your basic tray but you use a slim jewel case for the center piece.



    fig 3-6 by zakezuke

    To make your basic tray you'll need the following items


    fig 3-7 by zakezuke

    1. Template. See fig 3-3.

    2. Three pieces of cardboard or poster board measuring 131mm x 239mm, 131mm x 31mm, 131mm x 83mm

    3. One x-acto knife or equivalent

    4. Glue. General purpose or wood glue and contact cement or epoxy.

    5. Sanding block and 120grit general purpose paper.

    6. Tape

    7. Metric ruler

    8. Paper Chopper / optional.

    9. Black Magic marker

    10. Scrap CD

    11. Two pieces of tin foil or scrap paper measuring ½ inch square and ½ inch x 1½ inch





    fig 3-8 by zakezuke


    The slim jewel base is slightly wider than 131mm. Note fig 3-8. You'll notice that the center hub is off center. You can adjust alignment in software. The most important detail are the raised ridges. This is the most time consuming process. Also the center hub is too tall but this can be sanded down easily.

  1. Extract the slim jewel base from the clear cover.

  2. Break off the tabs that hold the clear cover.

  3. Using an X-acto knife or equivalent trim off the raised ridges so everything but the center hub is flush with the slim jewel base. It doesn't have to be perfect.

  4. Using 120grit sand paper on a sanding block sand down the 4 corners at a 45 degree angle to remove any imperfections with your cutting.

  5. Sand down the center hub with your sanding block in 10 stroke increments. Stop when you can place a CD in the base and have the center hub be flush.

  6. Sand down the the side shown left in fig 3-8 in 20 stroke increments. You'll need to remove about .5mm of tray. Stop when you can place your slim jewel base within the inner-cover CD tray slot on your printer.

  7. Print off a copy of digiguy's template linked from fig 3-3. Tape this to cardboard and using a ruler cut a rectangle measuring 131mm x 239mm.

  8. Cut out the three smaller 5.5mm holes into your cardboard.

  9. Using the template and a scrap CD, cut the circular hole for your CD.

  10. Keeping the template still taped to your cardboard turn upside down. Cut two pieces of tin foil or white paper ½ inch square and ½ inch x 1½ inch. Tape this to the bottom side of your top layer making sure the silver/white can be seen from the other side.

  11. Using a scrap CD as your guide, apply epoxy or contact cement to 4 corners not covered by the CD as well as the area to the left and right and apply to your cardboard making sure to align the center hub with digiguy's illustration. Any overlap can be sanded with your sanding block, and alignment can be adjusted with software. Apply a good and heavy object to the top of the slim jewel case and wait for the glue to dry.

  12. Once dry you'll need two more pieces of cardboard measuring 131mm x 31mm and 131mm x 83mm. See fig 3-7.

  13. Apply general purpose or wood glue to the bottom of your cardboard fore and aft of the slim jewel, smooth glue with paper towel. Apply the cardboard for and aft sections and place a heavy object and await to dry.

    See Section [7] for testing and alignment

  • Modifying an Epson Tray

    The Epson R200/R300 CD tray measures 135mm x 295mm where the Canon tray measures 131mm x 239mm. The width needs to be modified by 2mm on either side, and the nose needs to be trimmed by about 8mm. Length can be ignored.



    fig 3-9 by zakezuke

    Click above for full sized version

    For this you'll need


    1. Template. See fig 3-3.

    2. Metric ruler

    3. Sanding block with 80grit general purpose paper

    4. White Out or White oil based spray primer

    5. Trammels w/ cutoff wheel (optional)

    6. Hacksaw and miter box (optional).

    7. X-acto Knife or paper trimmer. (optional)


    1. Take your printout and trim per it's specifications. Cut out the 5.5mm holes at the top and tape to your Epson tray using the center hub to align. The edge near the beveled portion seen lower left in fig-3-9 should overlap by .5mm

    2. If you have a Dremmel with a cut off wheel use a piece of scrap wood as your guide and remove the front segment, and the overlap left and right. Users without a Dremmel may use a miter box and a hacksaw to perform the front cut of about 8mm, and using a sanding block sand down the left and right sides by 2mm. Users without a miter box will have to use an X-acto knife or equivalent and trim away the surplus on the front and sand smooth with 80grit paper.

    3. Paint on white reflectors by using digiguy's template as a stencil. You may use white out or white spray primer. Let dry. You may get rid of any overlap with your X-acto knife.

    4. Bevel the front slightly by sanding at a 45 degree angel, smooth the front and the sides with your sanding block. Check width by putting the tray
