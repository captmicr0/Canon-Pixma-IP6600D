[4]Setting the printer's destination

This section deals with setting the printer's “Destination” or country of origin to enable CD/DVD printing thanks to CanonFodder who found the Service manual.

  • Pixma iP series instructions.



    This information pertains to the iP3000, iP3000, iP4000, iP5000, iP6000D, and iP8500.


    1) Make sure printer is off and is plugged in


    2) Hold down [RESUME] (Triangle pointed down) hold down [POWER] and wait.


    3) Release [RESUME] but Continue to hold [POWER]. Double click [RESUME]. Release [POWER].

    4) Check to see you are in service mode by clicking [RESUME], than [POWER]. This should cause the printer to print a simple diagnostic that'll list it's ROM number and list CDR SENSOR=OK in red for the last line, whether the printer is set to US or not. pressing [RESUME] twice then [POWER] will print the ROM info. Note the color of “iPxxx (LTR) V1.xx”. Non-CDR support should be cyan. Keep this sheet to compare to the one after the you change the settings.


    5) Click the [RESUME] 5 times then click [POWER]. This is the Destination setting option. [orange]


    6)Pick a destination. Press [RESUME] this many times to pick one from the table below



    iP3000/4000/5000/6000D/8000

    iIP4200/iP5200/6600D WARNING – This information is unconfirmed! Try at own risk!




    1 time JPN (cyan)



    1 times IP4100 Japan

    2 times KOR (cyan)



    2 times Not Japan no CD-r support a4 paper

    3 times US (cyan)



    3 times Not Japan No CD-R support letter paper

    4 times EUR (cyan)



    4 times Not Japan CD-R support a4 paper

    5 times AUS (cyan)



    5 times Not Japan CD-R support letter paper

    6 times ASI (cyan)



    6 times you wish to abort and get back to the first menu

    7 times CHN (cyan)



    *4 times is recommended*

    8 times TIW (cyan)




    9 times JPN (pink)

    -contributed by rcaldjr2


    7) Press [POWER] to make the choice.


    8) Press [RESUME] then [POWER] again. This should cause the same test with ROM information but this time the “iPxxx (LTR) V1.xx” should be pink. See Fig-4-1.


    9) [optional] Press [RESUME] 7 times then [POWER] to perform a test print onto a printable CD-R


    10) Press [POWER] by it self to resume normal operation.

  • Pixma MP series instructions

Navigation through the service menus is done with the +/- buttons on the MP750/780 (see fig 4-2) or the right and left arrows on the MP760 (see fig 4-3). Note the Service Menu Map (fig 4-4).

This information pertains to the MP750, MP760, and MP780


1) Press [MENU] key, press [SCAN]] then [COPY] then the [SCAN] key again. This will result in the screen displaying “SERVICE MODE”


2) Navigate with the Right Arrow till you reach “#5 TYPE” and press [OK]

MP750 owners select CYGNUS-1 Press [OK]

MP780 owners select CYGNUS-2 Press [OK]

MP760 owners don't get this option

Use the Right arrow to select “STANDARD” and press [OK]

Press the power button to reset the printer DO NOT UNPLUG

Upon restarting the printer will ask your language preference. Select English or

your preferred choice.


3) Return to Service Mode as in step one

Navigate to “TEST MODE” using the left arrow. Press [OK]

Navigate to “PRINTER” using the right arrow.

Press [OK]

NOTE-see fig 4-4. This PRINTER is under the TEST MODE option.


Using the right arrow selection “[6] CD-R CALIBRATION” but before pressing [OK] make sure there is paper in the rear feeder. Without paper this step will silently fail. Once you are sure there is paper in the rear feeder press [OK]. A blank piece of paper is ejected. Next using the right arrow select “[4] EPROM while the printer is still in “3: PRINTER” sub menu. Press [OK]. This will result in a piece of paper being ejected with diagnostic information. Look for the line line that says CDRS=(XXX). If this reads (000) then alignment has failed and you need to repeat step three. If this reads something other than (000) then alignment is successful. Press [POWER] to reset the printer and resume normal operation.



This information is untested on the MP450, MP500, MP800, and MP950
