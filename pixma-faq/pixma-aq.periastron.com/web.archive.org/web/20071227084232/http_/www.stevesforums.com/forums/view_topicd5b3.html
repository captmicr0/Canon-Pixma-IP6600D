Depends on you if you can wait and spend around $30-50 for the OEM tray.

I made a plastic tray which works perfectly well. If you are ok not to print on 80 mm cd you can go ahead and do it yourself. It is hard to find a good plastic for reasonable price, but our "Amazing Savings" store sells decorative license plates for 50 cents. The size of these plastic plates are pretty lose to the tray. The only problem  is their color. So, I pained them in flat black and it works. The thing keep in mind the tray should be no more than 2 mm Also, it is good thing to have a central alignment support cut from the cd or dvd case to make cd better positioned during the print. This central support point will make life much easier when it comes to CD/dvd alignment. Otherwise, you have to be very precize in cutting the round shape to match the cd/DVD disk.  See my homemade plastic tray.

Finally made a tray that works. I followed digiguy's template and instructions. Thanks But I initially had problems as the tray was rejected. It turned out to be the reflectors. I placed black markings around the reflectors (made of aluminum foil), but the printer does not seem to detect them. What I did was to place masking tape around the reflectors and it worked. So I guess it's not the color around the reflectors that matters, it just has to be really nonreflective

I also use a Mac and confirm that no reinstallation of drivers is needed once the region is changed
