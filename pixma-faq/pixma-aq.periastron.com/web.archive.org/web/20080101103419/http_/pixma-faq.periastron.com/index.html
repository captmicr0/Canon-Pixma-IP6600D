CD printing on US model Pixmas

Canon first released a consumer inkjet supporting CD-printing in October, 2003. The i series, specifically the i865 and i965 employed the use of a separate CD printing tray guide (QM2-0626-000) that distinguished it from the i860 and i960 (PIXUS 990i in japan) models. See Druckerchannel.de (auf Deutch) for details. The Pixma iP series did away with a separate CD tray guide and incorporated it on all models but put a piece of blocking plastic for units that don't support CD/DVD printing.

North America is one market Canon decided not to include this feature. It's been suggested that Canon wished to avoid paying royalties to Phillips. Asking support about this results in the technically accurate but totally unhelpful answer, “Canon USA has made the decision not to include the CDR/DVD printing feature on consumer printers sold in this market.” But thinks to users of Steve's Forums it's been established that the iP3000, iP4000, iP5000, iP6000D, iP8500, MP750, MP760, and MP780 all can have this feature enabled with ease. The newer models like the iP4200, iP5200, iP6600D, MP450, MP500, MP800, and MP950 which were released the same month of this writing have less information on this subject.

US models *CAN* have the ability to print onto CDs except for these reasons

  • They have a piece of plastic blocking the inner cover, where CDr models have a slot with extra rollers.

  • CDr models include a tray

  • CDr models have the feature enabled on the printer.

  • The driver checks for this feature and stores whether it can print to CD-r in the registry

  • Non-CDr models do not include the software.

Users who do not wish to mess with their factory settings may enjoy buying a model directly from Europe or Asia, and deal with the voltage conversion if necessary . Otherwise all 5 of these conditions can be addressed with moderate ease.

These procedures have not been tested on the Canon Pixma iP4200, iP5200, iP6600D, MP450, MP500, MP800, and MP950.
