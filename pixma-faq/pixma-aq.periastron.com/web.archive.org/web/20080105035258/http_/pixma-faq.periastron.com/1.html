  1. Power

  • Power Supply

    According to arklab , “To my surprise, Expansys threw in a "Plug converter" which allows the huge UK plug to be used in a US wall socket. I have been using this exclusively for over a week now and no problems at all. I've checked the voltage and it is still 120v feeding into the printer.” According to information presently available Canon employs the use of auto-switching power supplies in their models with the exception of North American models. But, there is no information as to which part numbers support these voltages. So most users feel more comfortable replacing the power supply. See fig 1-1 for details


    fig 1-1

    Power Supply removal requires no tools. It is recommended to remove the ink-tanks and head assembly for this procedure.



    Look at the back of the printer and note the location of the two prong power connector. Turn printer upside down and look in the corder where the power cord is connected. You'll see a black rectangle with two clips fore and aft. Expanding these clips will release the power supply and reveal the three wire connection from the top of the power supply to the printer. Disconnect this three wire connector.



    Replacement is just the reverse of this process. Attach the idiot proof three wire power connector to the top of the power supply, point the two prong power cord connector to the rear of the printer, and slap new power supply in making sure to note when the printers power supply clips click to secure it.
